Fish-Fry





Ingredients:
500g firm white fish fillets (like cod, tilapia, or snapper), cut into 2-inch pieces
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
1/21 teaspoon coriander powder Â 
1/4 teaspoon garam masala
1 tablespoon lemon juice
Salt to taste
2-3 tablespoons rice flour or semolina (for coating)
2-3 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:
Marinate the fish: In a bowl, combine the fish pieces, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Mix well to ensure the fish is evenly coated. Rest the fish: Cover the bowl and let the fish marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. The longer it marinates, the more flavorful it will be. Prepare the coating: In a shallow dish, mix together the rice flour (or semolina) and all-purpose flour. Coat the fish: Take each marinated fish piece and dredge it in the flour mixture, ensuring it is evenly coated on all sides. Gently shake off any excess flour. Heat the oil: Heat a generous amount of vegetable oil in a shallow frying pan or skillet over medium-high heat. The oil should be hot enough for the fish to sizzle immediately when added. Fry the fish: Carefully place the coated fish pieces in the hot oil, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Fry in batches if necessary. Cook until golden: Fry the fish for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until it turns golden brown and is cooked through. The cooking time will depend on the thickness of the fish. Drain the excess oil: Once cooked, remove the fish from the pan using a slotted spoon and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to absorb any excess oil. Serve hot: Serve the fish fry hot with lemon wedges, onion slices, or your favorite chutney or sauce. Enjoy!
