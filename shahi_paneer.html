Shahi- Paneer




It is a delicious North Indian curry made with paneer (cottage cheese) and tomato based spicy gravy laced with spices. This is a traditional recipe that has been followed from generations and has been mainstay preparation in Indian cuisine since the time of Moguls. This shahi paneer recipe uses cashew nut based tomato gravy to bring in the rich texture and flavor
Ingredients:
For the Onion-Tomato Paste:
1 tablespoon ghee or oil
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1-inch cinnamon stick
2-3 green cardamom pods
1 black cardamom pod (optional)
2-3 cloves
1 bay leaf
1-2 green chilies, slit (adjust to your spice preference)
1-inch ginger, roughly chopped
3-4 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
2 medium onions, roughly chopped
10-12 cashew nuts, soaked in warm water for 15-20 minutes
2-3 medium tomatoes, roughly chopped
Salt to taste
1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
For the Shahi Paneer:
1-2 tablespoons butter
1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder (for color, optional)
1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
Prepared onion-tomato paste
250 grams paneer, cubed
1/4 cup fresh cream
1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed
Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish
A pinch of garam masala (optional)

Instructions:
1. Prepare the Onion-Tomato Paste:
Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat.
Add cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, green cardamom pods, black cardamom (if using), and cloves. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant. Add bay leaf and slit green chilies. Sauté for another minute.
Add chopped ginger and garlic. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn light golden brown.
Add soaked cashew nuts and sauté for a minute.
Add chopped tomatoes, salt, and turmeric powder. Mix well and cook until the tomatoes soften and the mixture leaves the sides of the pan. Let the mixture cool completely.
Once cooled, transfer the mixture to a blender and blend until you get a smooth paste. You can add a little water if needed to help with blending. For a very smooth gravy, you can strain the paste through a fine-mesh sieve.
2. Make the Shahi Paneer:
Heat butter in the same pan or a new pan over low to medium heat.
Add Kashmiri red chili powder and coriander powder. Sauté for a few seconds. Be careful not to burn the spices. Add the prepared onion-tomato paste to the pan. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the gravy thickens slightly. Add the paneer cubes and gently mix them with the gravy.
Add fresh cream and mix well.
Sprinkle crushed kasuri methi over the gravy. You can lightly crush it between your palms before adding.
Add a pinch of garam masala, if using.
Simmer for another 2-3 minutes on low heat, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors. Do not overcook the paneer, as it can become rubbery. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
3. Serve: Serve hot with naan, roti, paratha, or rice. Enjoy your delicious Shahi Paneer!
