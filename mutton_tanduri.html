Mutton Tandoori
This recipe yields a flavorful and tender mutton dish with a beautiful smoky char, perfect as a starter or a main course.
Ingredients:
For the Mutton:
500 grams mutton pieces (preferably leg or shoulder), cut into 2-inch cubes
2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
1 tablespoon lemon juice
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
1/4 teaspoon black salt (kala namak)
2 tablespoons thick yogurt (hung curd)
1 tablespoon mustard oil
1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed
Salt to taste

For the Second Marinade (Optional but Recommended):
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 tablespoon lemon juice
1 teaspoon red chili powder
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
1/4 teaspoon black salt  
2 tablespoons thick yogurt
1 tablespoon mustard oil
1/2 teaspoon kasuri methi
1/4 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
A pinch of orange food coloring (optional, for color)

Instructions:
First Marinade: In a large bowl, combine the mutton pieces, 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, black salt, 2 tablespoons of thick yogurt, 1 tablespoon of mustard oil, crushed kasuri methi, and salt to taste. Mix everything well, ensuring the mutton pieces are thoroughly coated with the marinade. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.   Second Marinade (Optional): In a separate bowl, prepare the second marinade by mixing 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, black salt, 2 tablespoons of thick yogurt, 1 tablespoon of mustard oil, kasuri methi, ajwain, and orange food coloring (if using). Remove the marinated mutton from the refrigerator. If you are using the second marinade, add it to the mutton and mix well, ensuring all pieces are evenly coated. Marinate for another 1-2 hours. Cooking: Tandoor (Traditional): Preheat your tandoor oven to a high temperature. Thread the marinated mutton pieces onto skewers. Place the skewers inside the hot tandoor and cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the mutton is cooked through and has a nice smoky char. Keep turning the skewers occasionally for even cooking. Oven Method: Preheat your oven to 220°C (430°F). Thread the marinated mutton pieces onto skewers and place them on a baking tray lined with parchment paper or a wire rack. Bake for 20-25 minutes, turning the skewers halfway through, until the mutton is cooked through and slightly charred. You can also broil (grill) for the last few minutes for a more pronounced char, keeping a close watch to prevent burning. Grill/Barbecue: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Lightly oil the grates. Thread the marinated mutton pieces onto skewers and grill for 15-20 minutes, turning frequently, until the mutton is cooked through and has grill marks. Serving: Once the mutton is cooked, remove it from the skewers. Garnish with lemon wedges, onion rings, and fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot as a delicious appetizer or main course. Enjoy your homemade Mutton Tandoori!
