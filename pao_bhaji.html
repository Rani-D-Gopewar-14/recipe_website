Pao Bhaji




Pao Bhaji Recipe
Pao Bhaji is a popular and flavorful street food from Mumbai, India, consisting of a spiced mashed vegetable curry (Bhaji) served with butter-toasted bread rolls (Pao). Here's a detailed recipe to make this delicious dish at home.
Ingredients
For the Bhaji (Vegetable Curry):
2 large potatoes, peeled and roughly cubed
1 cup cauliflower florets
½ cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
½ cup carrots, peeled and diced
½ cup capsicum (bell pepper), diced
2 medium onions, finely chopped
2-3 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
2 tablespoons Pav Bhaji Masala (easily available in Indian grocery stores)
½ teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
1 teaspoon coriander powder
½ teaspoon cumin powder
2-3 tablespoons butter
2 tablespoons oil
Salt to taste
Water, as needed
1-2 tablespoons lemon juice
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped, for garnish
For the Pao (Bread Rolls):
12-16 Pao (Indian bread rolls, similar to dinner rolls)
Butter, for toasting
Optional Garnishes:
Finely chopped onions
Fresh coriander leaves
A dollop of butter
Lemon wedges
Instructions
1. Prepare the Vegetables:
Wash and roughly chop the potatoes, cauliflower, carrots, and capsicum. If using fresh green peas, shell them. If using frozen, no need to thaw.

2. Boil the Vegetables:
In a large pot or pressure cooker, combine the cubed potatoes, cauliflower florets, green peas, and diced carrots. Add about 1-1.5 cups of water and salt to taste.
Pressure Cook: Cook for 3-4 whistles on medium heat. Let the pressure release naturally. Pot Cooking: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the vegetables are very tender and easily mashable (about 15-20 minutes). Drain any excess water, but reserve it.
3. Mash the Vegetables:
Once the vegetables are cooked, use a potato masher or a sturdy spoon to mash them well. You can leave some small chunks for texture if you prefer.

4. Sauté Onions and Tomatoes:
Heat oil and 1 tablespoon of butter in a large, heavy-bottomed pan or a large skillet over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown (about 8-10 minutes).
Add the ginger-garlic paste and finely chopped green chilies. Sauté for another minute until fragrant.
Add the finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate from the sides (about 5-7 minutes).
5. Add Spices:
Lower the heat and add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Sauté for about 30 seconds, stirring continuously to prevent burning.
6. Add Pav Bhaji Masala:
Add the Pav Bhaji Masala and sauté for another minute, allowing its aroma to release.

7. Combine Mashed Vegetables and Cook:
Add the mashed vegetables to the pan with the onion-tomato mixture. Mix well.
Add the reserved vegetable stock (if any) or some fresh water to adjust the consistency of the bhaji. It should be thick but slightly saucy. Add salt to taste (remember you added salt while boiling the vegetables). Bring the bhaji to a simmer and cook for another 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Mash any remaining large chunks of vegetables during this time.

8. Finish the Bhaji:

Stir in the lemon juice and 1-2 tablespoons of butter.
Garnish generously with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
Keep the bhaji warm over low heat.
9. Prepare the Pao:
Slice the pao horizontally, without cutting all the way through, so they are still connected on one side. Heat a flat griddle or pan over medium heat.
Spread butter generously on both the inner sides of the pao. Toast the pao on the hot griddle until they are lightly golden brown and crispy on both sides.
10. Serve:
Serve the hot bhaji in a bowl, garnished with a dollop of butter, chopped onions, and fresh coriander leaves (optional). Serve the butter-toasted pao alongside the bhaji. Offer lemon wedges for squeezing over the bhaji. Enjoy this classic and flavorful Pao Bhaji!
