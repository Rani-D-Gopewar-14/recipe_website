Fried Momos






Ingredients:
For the Momos (if you're not using store-bought):
1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
½ teaspoon salt
½ cup water (or as needed)
1 tablespoon oil
For the Filling:
1 cup finely chopped vegetables (cabbage, carrots, onions, bell peppers, etc.) OR 1 cup minced chicken or paneer (Indian cheese)
1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
2 tablespoons finely chopped coriander leaves
1 teaspoon soy sauce
½ teaspoon vinegar
¼ teaspoon black pepper powder
Salt to taste
1 tablespoon oil
For Frying:
Vegetable oil, for deep frying

Instructions:
1. Prepare the Momo Dough (if making from scratch):
In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth and firm dough. Add the oil and knead again. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

2. Prepare the Filling:
Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few seconds until fragrant. Add the chopped vegetables (or minced chicken/paneer) and sauté for 5-7 minutes until they are slightly softened. If using chicken, ensure it's cooked through. Add the green chilies, coriander leaves, soy sauce, vinegar, black pepper powder, and salt. Mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove the filling from the heat and let it cool completely.

3. Shape the Momos:
Divide the rested dough into small equal-sized balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter).1 Keep the edges thinner than the center.   Place a spoonful of the prepared filling in the center of the circle. Bring the edges of the dough together to seal the filling. You can shape them in various ways (crescent, pleated, etc.). Ensure the edges are tightly sealed to prevent the filling from coming out during frying.

4. Fry the Momos:
Heat enough vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium-high heat. The oil should be hot enough for frying but not smoking. Gently slide a few momos into the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the momos on medium heat, turning occasionally, until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Remove the fried momos with a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Repeat the process with the remaining momos.

5. Serve:
Serve the hot and crispy fried momos immediately with your favorite dipping sauces like momo chutney (spicy tomato-based sauce), schezwan sauce, or mayonnaise. Enjoy your homemade fried momos!
