Chicken Handi




Chicken Handi Recipe
Chicken Handi is a delicious and aromatic Indian chicken curry traditionally cooked in a clay pot ("handi"), which imparts a unique earthy flavor. However, you can easily make it in a regular heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven. This recipe provides instructions for a flavorful and creamy Chicken Handi.
Ingredients
For the Chicken Marinade:
500g chicken, cut into medium-sized pieces (bone-in or boneless thighs or breast)
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
1 teaspoon coriander powder  
½ teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
2-3 tablespoons plain yogurt
For the Curry:
2 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
1 large onion, finely chopped
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 black cardamom
2-3 green cardamom pods
2-3 cloves
1 inch cinnamon stick
1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
2-3 medium tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
½ teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
1 teaspoon coriander powder
½ teaspoon cumin powder
½ teaspoon garam masala  
½ cup plain yogurt, whisked
½ cup fresh cream or malai (optional, for extra richness)
1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed
2-3 green chilies, slit (optional)
2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves, for garnish
Salt to taste
Water, as needed

Instructions
1. Marinate the Chicken:
In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Add the yogurt and mix well, ensuring the chicken is evenly coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2-3 hours for deeper flavor.

2. Prepare the Base:
Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, black cardamom, green cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Let them splutter for a few seconds until fragrant. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown. This may take about 8-10 minutes. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.

3. Cook the Tomatoes and Spices:
Add the finely chopped or pureed tomatoes to the pot. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil starts to separate from the sides. This usually takes about 5-7 minutes. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Sauté for a minute, stirring continuously to prevent the spices from burning.

4. Cook the Chicken:
Add the marinated chicken to the pot and mix well with the tomato-onion masala. Cook on medium-high heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken starts to brown slightly.

5. Add Yogurt and Simmer:
Lower the heat to low. Add the whisked yogurt to the pot, stirring continuously to prevent it from curdling. Add salt to taste and mix well. Cover the pot and let the chicken simmer gently for 20-25 minutes, or until it is cooked through and tender. Stir occasionally and add a little water if the gravy becomes too thick.

6. Finish the Curry:
Stir in the garam masala and crushed kasuri methi. If using, add fresh cream or malai for a richer and creamier gravy. Mix well. Add the slit green chilies (if using). Simmer for another 5-10 minutes, uncovered, allowing the flavors to meld together. Check the seasoning and adjust salt or spices if needed.

7. Garnish and Serve:
Garnish the Chicken Handi with freshly chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, paratha, or steamed rice. Enjoy your flavorful and aromatic Chicken Handi!
