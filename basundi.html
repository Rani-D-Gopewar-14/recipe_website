Basundi Recipe (Sweetened Condensed Milk Dessert)
Basundi is a delicious and rich Indian dessert made by simmering milk until it thickens and reduces to about half its original volume. It's flavored with cardamom, nuts, and sometimes saffron. This recipe provides instructions for a classic and flavorful Basundi.
Ingredients
1 liter full-fat milk
¼ - ½ cup sugar (adjust to your sweetness preference)
¼ teaspoon cardamom powder
1 tablespoon slivered almonds
1 tablespoon slivered pistachios
A few strands of saffron (kesar) (optional)
Pinch of nutmeg powder (optional)

Instructions
1. Prepare the Milk:
Pour the full-fat milk into a heavy-bottomed pan or kadhai (Indian wok). Using a heavy-bottomed pan prevents the milk from scorching at the bottom.
2. Simmer the Milk:
Place the pan over medium heat and bring the milk to a boil, stirring occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the bottom. Once it boils, reduce the heat to low and let the milk simmer gently. Continue to stir frequently, scraping the sides of the pan to collect the solidified milk solids (malai) and incorporate them back into the milk. This process helps in thickening the Basundi and gives it a rich texture.
3. Reduce the Milk:
Continue simmering the milk on low heat until it has reduced to about half its original volume. This will take approximately 45-60 minutes, depending on the heat and the pan. The milk will become noticeably thicker and slightly creamy in color.

4. Add Sugar:
Once the milk has reduced sufficiently, add the sugar. Stir well until the sugar is completely dissolved. Continue to simmer for another 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, allowing the sugar to incorporate and the Basundi to thicken further. Taste and adjust the sweetness if needed.

5. Add Flavorings and Nuts:
Add the cardamom powder and mix well. If using saffron, soak the strands in a tablespoon of warm milk for a few minutes to release their color and flavor. Then, add the saffron milk to the Basundi. Add the slivered almonds and pistachios. Stir gently to combine. If using nutmeg powder, add a pinch and mix well.

6. Simmer and Cool:
Simmer the Basundi for another 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Remove the pan from the heat and let the Basundi cool down to room temperature. As it cools, it will thicken further.

7. Chill and Serve:
Once cooled, transfer the Basundi to a serving bowl or individual serving cups. Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours to chill thoroughly before serving. Garnish with a few more slivered nuts or saffron strands before serving, if desired. Enjoy this creamy and delicious Basundi as a dessert! It's often served during festivals and special occasions in India.
