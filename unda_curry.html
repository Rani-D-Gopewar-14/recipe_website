Unda Curry




Ingredients:
6 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and lightly pricked with a fork
2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
2 medium-sized tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
1 inch ginger, grated
3-4 cloves garlic, minced
1 green chili, slit (adjust to your spice preference)
1 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
1.51 teaspoons coriander powder  
1 teaspoon cumin powder
½ teaspoon garam masala
Salt to taste
2 tablespoons cooking oil
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish
½ cup water (or as needed for gravy consistency)
Instructions:

Prepare the Eggs: Boil the eggs until they are hard-boiled. Once cooled, peel them and lightly prick them all over with a fork. This helps the gravy to seep into the eggs. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a pan or pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. This might take about 8-10 minutes, so be patient. Add Ginger, Garlic, and Chili: Once the onions are browned, add the grated ginger, minced garlic, and slit green chili. Sauté for another minute until the raw smell of ginger and garlic disappears. Cook Tomatoes: Add the chopped tomatoes or tomato puree to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes are softened and the oil starts to separate from the sides. This usually takes about 5-7 minutes. Add Spice Powders: Lower the heat and add turmeric powder,

red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well and sauté for about a minute, ensuring the spices don't burn. You can add a tablespoon of water if the mixture seems too dry. Form the Gravy: Add salt to taste and about ½ cup of water. Bring the gravy to a simmer. You can adjust the amount of water depending on how thick you want your curry. Add Eggs and Simmer: Gently add the pricked hard-boiled eggs to the simmering gravy. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the eggs to absorb the flavors of the gravy. Finish with Garam Masala and Coriander: Sprinkle garam masala and fresh coriander leaves over the curry. Stir gently. Serve: Your Anda Curry is ready! Serve it hot with rice, roti, naan, or bread. Enjoy your delicious homemade Anda Curry! Canvas
