Steamed Momos




Ingredients:
For the Dough:
2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
½ teaspoon salt
¾ cup water, or as needed
For the Filling (Vegetarian Option):
1 cup finely chopped cabbage
½ cup finely chopped carrots
½ cup finely chopped onions
¼ cup finely chopped spring onions
1 tablespoon grated ginger
2-3 cloves garlic, minced
1-2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
1 tablespoon soy sauce
1 teaspoon vinegar
1 tablespoon vegetable oil
Salt and pepper to taste
(You can substitute the vegetarian filling with minced chicken, pork, or paneer as per your preference. If using meat, ensure it's very finely minced.)

Instructions:
1. Prepare the Dough:
In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth and firm dough. It should not be sticky. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

2. Prepare the Filling:
In a separate bowl, combine the chopped cabbage, carrots, onions, and spring onions. Add grated ginger, minced garlic, and chopped green chilies. Stir in the soy sauce, vinegar, and vegetable oil. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix everything well. If using meat or paneer, add it at this stage and mix thoroughly.

3. Shape the Momos:
Divide the rested dough into small, equal-sized balls (about 1 inch in diameter). Take one dough ball and roll it out into a thin circle (about 3-4 inches in diameter). Keep the edges thinner than the center. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of the rolled dough. Bring the edges together to form pleats and seal them tightly in your desired shape (crescent, round, or any other shape). Ensure there are no openings to prevent the filling from coming out during steaming. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.

4. Steam the Momos:
Bring about 2-3 cups of water to a boil in a steamer (a pot with a perforated tray or a tiered steamer). Grease the steamer tray with a little oil to prevent the momos from sticking. Arrange the momos on the greased steamer tray, leaving some space between them. Cover the steamer and steam for 10-15 minutes, or until the momos look translucent and the dough is cooked through. The steaming time may vary depending on the size and thickness of the momos and the filling used. Carefully remove the steamed momos from the steamer.

5. Serve:
Serve the hot steamed momos with your favorite spicy dipping sauces like tomato chutney, chili garlic sauce, or a traditional momo sauce. Enjoy your homemade steamed momos! Let me know if you have any other questions.
